All About Me!

James C. Liu

The Early Years 1985-2003
Hello Everyone! My name is James Liu, and this is my story. When I was three, my family moved to Los Angeles from Taipei, Taiwan. Like most other kids, my time was spent hanging out with friends, playing video games, and wondering why I was so average. Life was uneventful until 9/11. Shortly after, I left my sophomore year of high school to enlist in the Army.

So There I Was... 2003-2006
Life picked up speed after 2003. I deployed to Iraq that year shortly after completing basic training at the Infantry School in Ft. Benning, GA. Upon returning home, I was placed in charge of a platoon of 30 soldiers stationed at Ft. Lewis, WA and spent nearly a year leading them before I left the military and returned to school.

Life After the Military 2007-2012
I then wound up at the University of Southern California where I studied Philosophy, Business, and Engineering. It was during these years that I discovered my passion to help improve education. I also had a great experience as a Residential Advisor before leaving to conduct research projects in China and Ghana. Good times!

Life After School 2012-2013
After graduating, I moved to New York to work at an education-focused venture capital fund. It was an amazing two years and I left with great mentors, excellent experiences, and a head full of knowledge (not to mention a few grey hairs!). Having built a solid foundation of skills, it was time to go back to serving soldiers. Having completed the Army Intelligence School, I am now back in NY serving as the Electronic Warfare Officer for the 151 Theater Information Operations Group.

Onward! 2014
I am starting a new chapter in my life, and thanks to GA's Web Development Immersive program and the Opportunity Fund Scholarship, I am moving towards a career I've always wanted to pursue. I look forward to finally gaining the skills that will help me become a web developer. General Assembly - thank you so much for your time and for sharing my story!

From my Family to Yours - Please Have a Great Week!
